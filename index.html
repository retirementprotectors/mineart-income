The Bottom Line

At 5.0% return, these four accounts generate $3,292/mo in sustainable income without touching principal. That exceeds the $3,088/mo MHP gap by $204/mo. Stephen can retire from Mahaska Health and your lifestyle doesn't change.

Withdrawal Priority — What to Take and Why
1
Schwab Roth IRA — $196,887
Best first dollar. Every withdrawal is 100% tax-free — no federal, no state, no bracket impact. Stephen is 71, fully eligible, no penalties. Doesn't increase your taxable income, won't push you into a higher bracket, and won't trigger additional Social Security taxation.
Best First Dollar — Tax Free
2
Edward Jones NQ Brokerage — $200,000
Joint account, commission-based, fixed income portfolio. Only the gain portion is taxable — cost basis comes out tax-free. The fixed income holdings likely generate natural dividends and interest, reducing the need to sell positions. Minimal tax drag compared to IRA distributions.
Good Second Dollar — Favorable Tax
3
Joyce Schwab IRA — $133,629
Traditional IRA — every dollar withdrawn is ordinary income taxed at 22% federal (Iowa exempt). Joyce is 70, no early withdrawal penalty. Better to defer until RMDs start at age 73 (2029) since she'll be forced to take distributions then anyway. RMD at 73: approximately $5,042/yr ($420/mo).
Save for RMDs — Fully Taxable
4
North American VersaChoice10 — $426,802
NQ fixed indexed annuity, issued July 2024 — 10-year surrender schedule active. However, the standard 10% free withdrawal corridor allows up to ~$42,680/yr penalty-free. Our sustainable withdrawal at 5% is only ~$21,340/yr — well within the free corridor. Gain-first taxation (LIFO) at 22% federal. Largest account = largest income generator.
Largest Engine — Within Free Corridor
